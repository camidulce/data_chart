Internet: Connectivity changed the course of the Romanian presidential election



The connectivity and thus social media played a major role: democratic candidate's campaign was also fought online, with a massive mobilisation on Facebook and Twitter. After being elected, he became the first European politician to reach 1 million likes on Facebook, ahead of figures such as: Angela Merkel (898,444 likes), Nicolas Sarkozy ( 992,609 ), Francois Hollande ( 516,967 ), David Cameron, Sivio Berlusconi, Jose Manuel Barroso and Tony Blair. The likes jumped by 150,000 in 1 1/2 days.

The economy was a big election issue. The democratic candidate accused the socialist one (current PM) of driving Romania into recession. The PM riposted with figures from the statistical office (ahead of publication) showing that GDP rose by 1.9% in the third quarter over the previous one. He managed to hide the budget deficit this time. The most pressing challenge for the government is the budget for 2015, since the figures for this year were massaged to look good for the election (The Economist, 11/22/2014).

A measure of a Healthy Economy: GDP growth



The Gross Domestic Product (GDP) in Romania expanded 1.90 percent in the third quarter of 2014 over the previous quarter. GDP Growth Rate in Romania averaged 0.86 percent from 2000 until 2014, comparable to France. Not so spectacular when second quarter ended negative.

A measure of Wealth: GDP per Capita



Again, comparison is important. The gross domestic product per capita in Romania (the value of all final goods and services produced within a country in a given year, divided by the average (or mid-year) population for the same year) is a fraction of France or US's GDP.

Getting back to US: I looked for data at micro level and compared it for the two places where I lived: Chapel Hill, NC, and McLean, VA. Chapel Hill was definitively a 'bubble', just to realize that McLean is probably a padded one.

Final Project Conclusion:

It evolved steadily in one week and was dotted with constant pivoting due to limited accessible data sources.

Technologies:

First, it was a Ruby app: I thought it is important to persist the data in a database and be able to update it. However, after few days I pivoted to .csv files and a single-page presentation. In the process I used Ruby, Rails, Roo, APIs, then pivoted to HTML/CSS, .csv, python server to get over the ajax cross domain errors, jQuery and HighCharts.
A Tale of Three Countries...

...is a data visualization single page app which charts data in places I lived. The project stems from Romania's presidential elections from last November, when the socialist candidate boasted that the GDP rose during the last quarter of his mandate as a PM. This view was unilateral, as most of his campaign. The intent of this project is to show the importance of data comparison.

Here, I plot multiple data sets and compare data points in places I lived, 'delicate arch' from raw data to its visual representation.
